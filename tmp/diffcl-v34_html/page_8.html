  1. The Effectiveness of the MSI for Adaptive Generation of Boundary-Refining Samples:

As shown in Fig. 9, removing MSI results in looser and more fragmented clusters in the t-SNE space. The generated features with MSI (Fig. 9(b)) exhibit better inter-class separability and tighter intra-class cohesion. Moreover, the feature space visualizations in Fig. 9(c)-(d) demonstrate that models trained without MSI yield disorganized embedding distributions, whereas enabling MSI leads to more structured and class-aligned representations. These qualitative insights are corroborated by the quantitative comparison in Table XI, where MSI improves KNN accuracy from 0.8985 to 0.9806, and boosts ARI and NMI by over $16\%$ and $11\%$ respectively. Additionally, the IDR increases to 8.0715, indicating improved cluster compactness and boundary sharpness.
