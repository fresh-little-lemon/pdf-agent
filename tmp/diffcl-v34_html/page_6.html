C. Evaluation Criteria

Classification performance was quantified using Top-1 Accuracy [28], macro-averaged Recall [42], F1-score [42], Area Under the Receiver Operating characteristic Curve (AUROC) [10], and Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve (AUPRC) [10]. Calibration quality was evaluated through the Brier Score [3] and Negative Log-Likelihood (NLL) [35]. Clustering efficacy was assessed using the Silhouette Score [39], Adjusted Rand Index (ARI) [25], Normalized Mutual Information (NMI) [47], and the Inter/Intra-cluster Distance Ratio(IDR). Qualitative evaluation included Confusion Matrix analysis, t-SNE [46] visualizations of feature embeddings, and confidence distribution plots via Violin charts [20].

D. The Superiority Analysis of DiffCL for Defect Classification

We conducted a performance evaluation of DiffCL using two industrial defect benchmark datasets: the In-house Steel dataset and the NEU-CLS dataset. These datasets were benchmarked against eight representative contrastive learning methods: SimCLR [6], SupCon [26], HCL [38], DCL [8], FairKL [2], Decoupled [52], ADNCE [50], and GCA [7]. To ensure experimental fairness, all methods were implemented under identical training configurations, including data partitions. Additionally, two direct classification baselines TipAdapter [55] and EMO [54] were used for the In-house Strip Steel dataset to assess the performance in high-difficulty classification scenarios. For the relatively simpler NEU-CLS [19] dataset, we employed Simple, a lightweight baseline model with a four-layer convolutional architecture, ensuring consistency and balanced evaluation across all tasks.
